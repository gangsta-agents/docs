# Glossary

Gangsta uses mafia terminology throughout the framework. Here's what every term means in plain English — and why it maps to its software development concept.

::callout{type="info" icon="i-lucide-book-open"}
Terms are listed alphabetically. Each entry explains the **real mafia meaning** and the **Gangsta meaning**.
::

---

## A

### Associate

**Mafia:** Someone who works with a crime family but isn't a full member. They're trusted helpers — contractors, not employees.

**Gangsta:** External tools and API proxies. Not part of the agent hierarchy — they provide services that Soldiers and Capos use. Examples: GitHub API, database clients, CI/CD systems.

---

## B

### Borgata

**Mafia:** Italian word for a mafia family or organization — the whole crew together.

**Gangsta:** The complete agent hierarchy — Don, Consigliere, Underboss, Capos, Soldiers, and Associates operating as one structured team. When someone says "The Borgata runs this," they mean the full agent system is in play.

---

## C

### Capo

**Mafia:** Short for *caporegime* — a captain who commands a crew of soldiers. Reports to the Underboss.

**Gangsta:** A domain crew lead. Each Capo owns a **territory** (a specific area of the codebase) and orchestrates Soldiers within it. Invoked via `gangsta:the-capo`.

### Checkpoint

**Mafia:** Not a mafia term — but in any operation, it's a control point where you verify status before proceeding.

**Gangsta:** A state file written to disk at the end of each Heist phase. Checkpoints are what make Heist resumption possible — any session can read the checkpoint and pick up where the last one left off.

### Consigliere

**Mafia:** The boss's trusted advisor. Not in the chain of command — operates outside it to give unbiased counsel. The word is Italian for "counselor."

**Gangsta:** The strategic advisor — an impartial agent for architectural review, security auditing, and code assessment. Because they're outside the chain of command, they give honest assessments rather than pleasing the hierarchy. Invoked via `gangsta:the-consigliere`.

### Constitution

**Mafia:** The family's internal rules — what's allowed, what's forbidden, how disputes are settled.

**Gangsta:** Accumulated project-specific rules stored in The Ledger at `docs/gangsta/constitution/`. The Constitution grows over time as Heists add lessons learned. It extends Omerta with project-level constraints.

### Contract

**Mafia:** A murder contract — a formal agreement that a hit will happen. Once signed, it's binding.

**Gangsta:** The formal specification document produced during [The Sit-Down](/heist-pipeline/the-sit-down). Signed by the Don, it defines scope, acceptance criteria, architectural decisions, and rules. No Soldier writes code without one. If code contradicts the Contract, you revise the Contract — not the code (Omerta Law #5).

### Cowboy Coding

**Mafia:** Not a mafia term — slang for a reckless outlaw who operates without discipline or rules.

**Gangsta:** Undisciplined development without structure — writing code without specs, skipping phases, flying solo without hierarchy. Gangsta exists to eliminate cowboy coding. The opposite of how The Borgata operates.

---

## D

### Devil's Advocate

**Mafia/General:** The role of someone who argues the opposite position to stress-test an idea — not their real opinion, but a structured challenge.

**Gangsta:** One of two voices in [The Grilling](/heist-pipeline/the-grilling). The Devil's Advocate challenges every assumption made by the Proposer, surfaces risks, and forces edge-case thinking. Their job is not to win — it's to make the final approach stronger.

### Don

**Mafia:** The boss. The head of the family. Supreme authority — no one outranks the Don.

**Gangsta:** You. The user. The Don approves every phase gate, signs every Contract, and declares every Heist complete. The framework never auto-advances without your explicit approval. Your word overrides everything — including the skills.

### Dossier

**Mafia/Intelligence:** A file of collected intelligence about a target — surveillance notes, known associates, patterns of behavior.

**Gangsta:** The output of [Reconnaissance](/heist-pipeline/reconnaissance) — a structured report of codebase intel including file structure, dependencies, test coverage, and relevant Ledger entries. The Don approves the Dossier before the Heist moves to The Grilling.

---

## F

### Fail

**Mafia:** Not a mafia term.

**Gangsta:** A Ledger entry documenting a mistake — an approach that caused rework, confusion, or wasted effort. Fails are preserved so they're never repeated. Written to `docs/gangsta/fails/`.

---

## G

### The Grilling

**Mafia:** A police interrogation — pressure applied until the truth comes out.

**Gangsta:** Phase 2 of the Heist Pipeline. An adversarial debate where a Proposer argues for the best approach and a Devil's Advocate challenges every assumption. The pressure surfaces edge cases and risks that single-perspective design misses. Output: Grilling Conclusions. Skill: `gangsta:the-grilling`.

---

## H

### Heist

**Mafia:** A carefully planned robbery. Multiple roles, coordinated phases, a clear objective, and (ideally) a clean getaway.

**Gangsta:** The complete 6-phase development cycle for any feature: [Reconnaissance](/heist-pipeline/reconnaissance) → [The Grilling](/heist-pipeline/the-grilling) → [The Sit-Down](/heist-pipeline/the-sit-down) → [Resource Development](/heist-pipeline/resource-development) → [The Hit](/heist-pipeline/the-hit) → [Laundering](/heist-pipeline/laundering). The Don approves each phase gate. No shortcuts, no skipped phases.

### The Hit

**Mafia:** Carrying out a murder — the actual execution of the plan.

**Gangsta:** Phase 5 of the Heist Pipeline — the actual code execution. Soldiers implement the War Plan in parallel, following Test-Driven Development per the Contract. Budget tributes are tracked. Skill: `gangsta:the-hit`.

---

## I

### Insight

**Mafia:** Not a mafia term.

**Gangsta:** A Ledger entry preserving a successful pattern, non-obvious approach, or domain knowledge worth reusing. Written to `docs/gangsta/insights/` during Laundering or after significant discoveries.

---

## L

### Laundering

**Mafia:** Money laundering — running dirty money through legitimate businesses until it comes out clean and untraceable.

**Gangsta:** Phase 6 of the Heist Pipeline — verification and integration. The Consigliere reviews all deliverables against the Contract, integration tests run, The Ledger is updated with insights and fails, and the output is clean, production-ready code. Skill: `gangsta:laundering`.

### The Ledger

**Mafia:** An accounting book tracking debts, payments, and who owes what. The family's financial memory.

**Gangsta:** Gangsta's institutional memory — stored in `docs/gangsta/`. Contains Insights (successful patterns), Fails (documented mistakes), and the Constitution (accumulated rules). Unlike a session, The Ledger persists. Skill: `gangsta:the-ledger`.

---

## O

### Omerta

**Mafia:** The mafia's code of silence — the rule that you never talk to law enforcement, never betray the family, and never act outside sanctioned channels. Violating Omerta is punishable by death.

**Gangsta:** The five governance laws that prevent chaos in AI-assisted development: Introduction Rule, Rule of Availability, Rule of Truth, Rule of Tribute, and Spec is Law. Always active. Non-negotiable. Skill: `gangsta:omerta`. See [Omerta — The Five Laws](/core-concepts/omerta).

---

## P

### Phase Gate

**Mafia/Military:** A control point in an operation where you verify you're ready before proceeding to the next step.

**Gangsta:** The mandatory checkpoint at the end of each Heist phase. The Don must explicitly approve before the next phase begins. There is no auto-advancing — ever. If the output isn't right, the phase is rejected and redone.

---

## R

### Reconnaissance

**Military/Criminal:** Scouting an area before an operation — gathering intelligence about the target, its defenses, and the environment.

**Gangsta:** Phase 1 of the Heist Pipeline. Associates survey the codebase, identify patterns, read documentation, and check The Ledger. The output is the Dossier. Skill: `gangsta:reconnaissance`.

### Resource Development

**Mafia:** Assembling the crew, securing equipment, and planning the operation before the heist.

**Gangsta:** Phase 4 of the Heist Pipeline. The Contract is decomposed into parallel work packages, territories are assigned to Capos, and tributes (resource budgets) are estimated. The output is the War Plan. Skill: `gangsta:resource-development`.

---

## S

### Shadow Hotfix

**Mafia:** Not a mafia term — a shadow operation is one done without authorization from above.

**Gangsta:** Code written outside the Heist Pipeline — without a spec, without a Contract, without Don approval. The equivalent of a rogue Soldier acting on their own. Violates Omerta Law #5 (Spec is Law). The framework is designed to make this impossible.

### The Sit-Down

**Mafia:** A formal meeting between family heads or senior members to settle disputes, make agreements, or plan operations. Everyone comes to the table.

**Gangsta:** Phase 3 of the Heist Pipeline. The Contract is drafted — no code allowed. This phase is purely specification. If any code appears, the phase restarts. The Don signs the Contract before proceeding. Skill: `gangsta:the-sit-down`.

### Soldier

**Mafia:** A made member of the family — a full member who's been formally inducted. Reports to a Capo, executes orders, and doesn't freelance.

**Gangsta:** A stateless subagent that executes assigned tasks. Soldiers implement code per the Contract and War Plan, follow TDD (`gangsta:drill-tdd`), and report completion. They don't make architectural decisions — they execute.

### Spec is Law

**Mafia:** Not a mafia term, but it maps to the idea that a signed contract between families is binding — you don't renegotiate after the fact.

**Gangsta:** Omerta Law #5. The Contract (spec) is the authoritative source of truth. If code contradicts the spec, you revise the spec — not the code. Code is never the primary artifact. Implementation reveals a spec issue? Stop, propose a Contract revision, get Don approval, then resume.

### Stronzate

**Italian:** An Italian profanity meaning "bullshit" — nonsense, garbage, things that aren't true.

**Gangsta:** Used in Omerta's Rule of Truth context: "Uncited = stronzate." Any claim about the codebase or architecture that doesn't cite a specific file, line number, or source is stronzate — and not acceptable. The Rule of Truth exists to prevent hallucination from propagating through agent chains.

---

## T

### Territory

**Mafia:** A geographic or business area controlled by a mob family. Stepping on another family's territory causes conflict.

**Gangsta:** A domain of the codebase assigned to a Capo during Resource Development. Territories prevent Soldiers from stepping on each other's work — each Capo owns their domain and coordinates within it.

### Tribute

**Mafia:** A regular payment made to the family in exchange for protection or operating rights. Everyone pays tribute up the chain.

**Gangsta:** The resource budget allocated to a Soldier's task during Resource Development. Tributes are tracked during The Hit — tasks that exceed their tribute must flag it to the Don. This is Omerta's Rule of Tribute: resource budgets are tracked, not ignored.

---

## U

### Underboss

**Mafia:** The second-in-command of a crime family. Handles day-to-day operations, manages Capos, and acts when the Don is unavailable.

**Gangsta:** The Chief Operating Officer of The Borgata. Handles task decomposition, resource allocation, and planning. Dispatches work packages to Capos and Soldiers. The operational layer between the Don's vision and the crew's execution. Skill: `gangsta:the-underboss`.

---

## W

### War Plan

**Military/Criminal:** A detailed operational plan for a campaign — who does what, when, with what resources, and in what sequence.

**Gangsta:** The output of Resource Development. The Contract decomposed into specific tasks, with Soldiers assigned, territories claimed, and tribute estimates set. The Don approves the War Plan before The Hit begins. Stored at `docs/gangsta/{heist-name}/plans/`.

---

## The 1% Rule

**Gangsta-specific:** If there's even a 1% chance a skill applies to what the agent is doing, it must be invoked. No rationalizing. No "this is probably simple enough to skip." The rule exists because agents are prone to convincing themselves that structure isn't needed — and that's exactly when it is.

See: [The 1% Rule](/getting-started/the-1-percent-rule)
